# Legibility as the Root of All Evil

This post has been sitting in my drafts for a while with little more than this title. But the idea has been clear to me for sometime. From when I [started my software career](https://jimmyhmiller.github.io/ugliest-beautiful-codebase) until just a few years ago, the choices that businesses made for software projects had baffled me. Every company I had worked at paid lip service to efficiency, but at every turn actively created processes that hindered it greatly. I remember multiple occassions (some of which I was on the receiving end of), where people were yelled at for "finishing work early". When I would tell these stories to non-software engineers, they would question me almost in disbelief. Surely they weren't yelled at for doing the "right work" early? They must have done something wrong. But no, unfortunately for them those software engineers learned the lesson we all must learn at enterprise software companies, efficiency, speed, velocity, whatever you want to call it, are values in name only, not in reality.

It was many years later when reading Seeing Like a State that everything clicked for me. These companies value legibility more than anything. Being now much further along in my career I could see that clearly
