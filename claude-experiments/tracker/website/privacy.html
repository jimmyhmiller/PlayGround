Ease Support Privacy

Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-05-17

Ease is a macOS application developed by Jimmy Miller. This document explains what data Ease collects, where it lives, and what we do with it. The short version: we don't collect anything.

What data Ease handles

Ease stores the goals you create and the entries you log against those goals. This data is written to your local Mac. If you sign in to iCloud and have iCloud Drive enabled, Ease synchronizes that data to your private CloudKit database so it's available on your other Macs.

Your goals and entries are encrypted in transit and at rest by Apple's iCloud infrastructure. They are stored in your iCloud account; we have no access to them.

What Ease does not do

  • No analytics, no telemetry, no crash reporting.
  • No accounts, no email collection.
  • No advertising or third-party SDKs.
  • No data shared with anyone, ever.

Updates (website version only)

The version of Ease sold from this website checks for updates by fetching an appcast file from GitHub. This is an anonymous HTTPS request that includes the standard user agent your operating system sends; no identifying data is included. You can disable auto-update checks from the menu bar context menu.

The version of Ease distributed through the Mac App Store receives updates through the App Store itself; no network request originates from Ease for updates.

License keys

If you purchased Ease from this website, your license key may be verified with our reseller (Lemon Squeezy). The activation request contains the license key and a generated machine identifier, and is sent over HTTPS to api.lemonsqueezy.com. We do not store this information on our own servers.
